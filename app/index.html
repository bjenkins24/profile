This is the landing page for creating a free teacher profile at YourMusicLessons. Only an email address is required to get started. A password is created later when the teacher's email address is confirmed. Click here to see the full page.

Teachers that start but do not finish their profile go automatically on drip email campaign urging them to finish.

After the prospective teacher enters their email, they are directed to enter information for their profile. A modal popup appears with instructions on how to continue.

By clicking on "View Your Profile" teachers can see what their profile is looking like as they enter information without reloading the page.

There are multiple sections that the teacher can edit on their profile at this stage including: instruments they teach, levels, ages, their contact information, bio, photo, locations, and prices.

After finishing the required fields, teachers are taken to this page where they must enter some availability to teach. They simply click and drag to add availability. Teachers may also choose which location they are available for the block they are adding.

In the past, teachers could choose a city or an address and a travel radius around that address for each availability block. This feature was taken out as it was rarely used and overcomplicated the UI.

There is some information that teachers must know before teaching with YourMusicLessons. Many teachers often don't talk to a human before starting to teach, so this quiz is a way to make sure they have the information they need.

Above each set of questions is a few paragraphs with all the answers to the questions. Most teachers will not read the paragraphs of explaining text, but in order to continue, they must answer the questions correctly. Even guessing the answers will give the teachers the necessary information.

A contract must be signed to finalize the independent contractor relationship. This is embedded by legalesign.

Originally I used Adobe Sign (EchoSign) to send contracts, but in order to have API access or embed a signature form, the costs were too high so I went with legalesign and they have worked out well.

After the contract is signed, the teacher's profile is live on YourMusicLessons and it alerts and admin for review.

The profile score uses gamification to give teachers insentive to fill out more of their profile. The profile score can also be used in search results to make sure the most complete profiles are coming up near the top.

An audio recording is featured near the top of each profile.

In order to make it as easy as possible for each teacher to have a recording, I created an automated phone system to record the teacher. The teacher just calls in and records their voice like a voicemail and it automatically appears on the website.

Teachers can add many sections to their profiles including photos, videos, education, awards, get background checked through a third party, add a faq, languages, and an unlimited amount of custom sections.

Students can write a review for a teacher by clicking "write review" at the bottom of the teacher's profile. Current students with YourMusicLessons automatically get the "Verified Student" tag.

Reviews are displayed on the teacher's profile after the reviewer's email is verified, or immediately if the reviewer is a current student at YourMusicLessons and is logged in.

The entire YourMusicLessons accounting system was first custom created. This is the chart of accounts. All of the accounts journal entries are available when "view" is clicked.

New accounts can be created by filling out this form. They are immediately added to the Chart of Accounts and the correct associations are made.

The majority of entries are based off of payments, lessons, and refunds. These are automatically made when teacher's log lessons in the system and payments are received or made to teachers.

For general purchases this manual entry form may be used.

This is the double entry journal for a single account. It can be filtered based on date and it can be exported to a CSV file as well.

All fields are editable by double clicking the field, similar to an excel spreadsheet.

Available financial statements include the balance sheet, income/profit and loss statement, cash flow, and trial balance.

Each statement is filterable by date.

This is the customer's billing page. A running balance of each debit and credit is given directly on this page.

If a customer signs up for Auto Pay their account is refilled only when there balance can't pay for another lesson. That way only students that are actively taking lessons continually get charged with auto pay.

This is the screen for making a manual payment. If students aren't subscribed to auto pay they must make a manual payment online at least 24 hours before their scheduled lesson.

If they do not, the teacher is alerted and can cancel the lesson.

Each student is set on a schedule and lessons are automatically added to the system based on that schedule. Once a lesson time has passed the teacher must log in and mark if the lesson was given at the time it was scheduled.

If the lesson was not given, the teacher must select the reason for why it was not given. If a student quits, the teacher can also mark that here.

For key selections, like quitting, the student is either automatically followed up with via email or they are added to the follow up queue for me to personally reach out to them later.

If the lesson wasn't given, but was going to be rescheduled the teacher can mark that here as well. If it's a permanent change, all future lessons will change time according to the newly selected schedule.

After a teacher logs a lesson, it is added both as a charge to the student and as a payment to the teacher.

Payments to teachers go out automatically at midnight every Monday by ACH through Dwolla. If the teacher gives a lesson to a student that hasn't paid yet, the payment to the teacher is withheld until the student makes the payment.

Each lesson is itemized on the payment page. The payment to the teacher is calculated based on the cost of the lesson (which the teacher sets) and the cut that YourMusicLessons takes.
